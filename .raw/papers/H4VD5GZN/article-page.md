Title: Opportunities for flexible electricity loads such as hydrogen production from curtailed generation

Journal: Advances in Applied Energy, Volume 3, 25 August 2021, Article 100051

DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.adapen.2021.100051

Authors: Tyler H. Ruggles, Jacqueline A. Dowling, Nathan S. Lewis, Ken Caldeira

Open access status: article page states `open access` under a Creative Commons license.

Highlights captured by snapshot:

- Least-cost electricity systems have unused generation capacity during most hours.
- Flexible loads can initially be added to systems with minimal generation expansion.
- Initially, flexible loads may operate at low marginal cost with high capacity factors.
- Increasing flexible loads beyond about 25% of total load accelerates generation expansion.
- Increasing electricity system flexibility lessens unused and/or curtailed generation.
